Design of a Fuzzy KB Supported by Fingrams

Generate, Visualize, and Analyze Fingrams

This step allows to create and analyze fingrams from the system previously induced. Fingrams are graphical representations of the system. The rules are represented by nodes, and the links represent the relations among rules in terms of cofiring degree. Moreover, the graph is simplified keeping the most relevant relations through the use of Pathfinder algorithm.
The first window shows the different fingrams available along with a summary of quality indicators related to the fuzzy system under design. The top part of the window allows to select the fingrams that can be viewed, while the inferior part gives information about system quality, and provides a legend aimed at making easy to understand the information embedded into fingrams.
Three fingrams are created: one complete with all the original links (Original Fingram), one pruned from the original one (PFNET: Consistency and Redundancy), and one showing just the potential inconsistency among rules (PFNET: Only Consistency).
In case you click "DO", then the generated fingrams are automatically displayed. It is easy to appreciate how rules R1, R23, R35 and R37 can be removed without jeopardizing accuracy (it is only slightly reduced). You can remove such rules just by clicking on them, in one of the displayed fingrams. As a result, such rules are deactivated in the list of rules while fingrams are updated and displayed again. Of course, you can activate them again later.
STEPS SOFTWARE:
1. Open the Expert View (if needed)
2. Click on button Fingrams
3. Select the desired fingrams to show in the superior panel
4. Press the button Apply to show those fingrams
5. Then, the fingrams can be zoomed, moved, printed or exported
6. Click on the rules you would like to remove (R1, R23, R35 and R37 in our case)
